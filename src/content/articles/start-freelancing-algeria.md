Freelancing from Algeria is one of the best ways to earn in hard currency. But starting is confusing: which platform? What skills? How much to charge? How to get paid?

I went from zero to full-time freelancer in 6 months. Here's the exact path I'd recommend for someone starting today.

## Step 1: Pick One Skill (Not Three)

Most beginners try to offer everything. "I build websites, design logos, write copy, and edit videos." Result: you're good at nothing and clients sense it.

Pick one skill that:
- Has demand in the Gulf market (see step 2)
- You can learn to a professional level in 3 months
- You enjoy doing (you'll do it a lot)

**Best bets for Algerian freelancers in 2026:**
- Web development (Next.js, basic React)
- Web design (Figma to code)
- Mobile app development (Flutter)
- Content writing (English or Arabic)
- Social media management for businesses

I chose web development because it pays the most and has the most demand.

## Step 2: Learn to a Professional Level

"Professional level" means you can build a complete project without following a tutorial. Not expert, not senior -- just competent.

**How to get there:**
1. Follow one complete course on your skill (Udemy, YouTube, free resources)
2. Build 2 projects from scratch (no tutorial, just specs)
3. Show them to someone who works in the field and ask for honest feedback
4. Fix the issues they identify

**Time estimate:** 2-3 months of consistent effort. 2-3 hours/day.

## Step 3: Create Your Portfolio

You need proof that you can do the work. If you don't have client projects, build for friends, family, or local businesses for free (see [this article](/articles/how-to-get-first-freelance-client/)).

**Your portfolio needs:**
- 2-3 projects (quality over quantity)
- Each one with: problem, solution, result (see [portfolio guide](/articles/freelance-portfolio-problem/))
- A simple website or a Mostaql profile

## Step 4: Choose Your Platform

**Mostaql** is the best starting point for Algerian freelancers targeting the Gulf. Arabic interface, regional clients, reasonable rates.

Create a profile with:
- Professional photo (clear, well-lit)
- Skills section (be specific, not generic)
- Portfolio (the 2-3 projects from Step 3)
- Starting rate: $20-30/hour (competitive for beginners)

## Step 5: Get Your First Client

Bid on 5 projects per day on Mostaql. Focus on projects where:
- The budget is modest ($100-300)
- The requirements are clear (not vague)
- Fewer than 5 proposals submitted (easier to win)

For your proposal:
- Address the client by name
- Show you read their requirements
- Suggest a specific approach
- Quote a fixed price (not hourly)
- Include a link to your portfolio

**Bid on 50 projects before evaluating.** Most beginners give up after 10. The first client is the hardest. After that, referrals and repeat clients fill your pipeline.

## Step 6: Get Paid

For your first few projects, use Mostaql's payment system or ask for USDT via Binance P2P (see [this guide](/articles/business-without-bank-account/)).

Always require 50% upfront. This filters unserious clients and covers your costs if the project falls through.

## Step 7: Raise Your Rates

After 3 successful projects with good reviews, raise your rates by 50%. Then again after 10 projects. If you're not raising rates every 3-6 months, you're working below your market value.

## Timeline

- Month 1-2: Learn the skill (2-3 hours/day)
- Month 3: Build portfolio projects
- Month 4-5: Bid on Mostaql, get first client
- Month 6: Raise rates, get consistent work
- Month 12: Full-time freelancing or beyond

## FAQs

### Q: Do I need a degree to freelance?
A: No. Clients care about what you can build, not what degree you have.

### Q: How much can I realistically earn in my first year?
A: $300-800/month in months 4-6. $800-2,000/month in months 7-12. More if you specialize.

### Q: What equipment do I need?
A: A laptop (any), internet connection, and a quiet space. That's it.

### Q: Should I work in English or Arabic?
A: Both. Arabic for Mostaql and Gulf clients. English for Upwork and global clients.
