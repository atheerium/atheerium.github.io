I've talked to dozens of Algerian small business owners about software. Almost all of them use free tools (WhatsApp, Google Sheets, Canva) because paid SaaS is priced for US and European markets.

A $29/month Shopify subscription is $29. For a US business, that's about one hour of minimum wage. For an Algerian business, that's 4,500 DZD -- a significant expense when your average order value is 2,000 DZD.

This gap is both a problem and an opportunity. The problem is that existing tools don't fit. The opportunity is that tools priced for the market can win without competing on features.

## The Problem: Global Pricing Doesn't Fit Local Economics

Here's the math problem:

- US SaaS charges $50/month (average for small business tools)
- $50 = ~7,500 DZD at current rates
- Average Algerian small business profit: 100,000-200,000 DZD/month
- $50/month SaaS = 4-8% of monthly profit just for one tool

Now add that they need multiple tools: accounting, inventory, marketing, email, project management. A US business spends $200-500/month on SaaS. That's 0.5-1% of revenue. An Algerian business spending the same amount is spending 5-10% of revenue. It doesn't work.

The result: most Algerian businesses don't pay for software. They use free-tier tools, hack together workarounds, and avoid the expense. The market isn't "price sensitive" -- it's "priced out."

## What Pricing Works

Based on what I've seen work (my own tools and others that have traction in Algeria):

**For consumer tools:** Free, or under 500 DZD/month ($3). Anything above this and you lose 80% of the market.

**For business tools (SMB):** 1,000-2,000 DZD/month ($6-13). At this price, a business evaluates on value, not cost.

**For enterprise (10+ employees):** 5,000-10,000 DZD/month ($30-65). At this scale, they have the revenue to justify it, but they expect support and customization.

My own pricing follows this: $3-15/month for products aimed at individual freelancers and small teams. At $15/month, I need 10 customers to cover my costs. At $50/month, I need 3 -- but I'd have trouble finding those 3 in Algeria.

## What to Build Instead

Don't compete with Shopify. Compete with the absence of a solution.

An Algerian business using WhatsApp + Google Sheets + a notebook isn't going to switch to a $200/month ERP. But they will pay $10/month for a tool that:
- Replaces the notebook (one specific problem)
- Works in Arabic or French
- Supports local payment methods (COD, Baridimob, CCP)
- Integrates with local delivery companies (Yalidine, Noest)

The tool doesn't need 100 features. It needs 5 features that solve the exact problems they have right now.

**Examples of tools the Algerian market needs:**

- **Delivery price calculator** for Yalidine/Noest by wilaya (currently done by phone lookup)
- **WhatsApp order management** (currently done by scrolling through chat history)
- **Product catalog with local currency** (currently done via Facebook album)
- **Inventory tracking** for small shops (currently done in a notebook)
- **Client debt tracking** (extremely common in Algeria -- someone always owes money)

Each of these is a narrow tool, not a platform. Build narrow, price local, win the market.

## How You Can Do It Too

If you're a developer looking to build for the Algerian market:

1. **Watch how people work.** Go to a shop, a delivery office, a small factory. Watch them use WhatsApp and notebooks. That friction is your product opportunity.

2. **Start at $5-10/month.** Price testing is easier when you start low and raise. It's harder when you start high and lower.

3. **Accept COD payments.** Most businesses won't use a credit card. Offer monthly via CCP, Baridimob, or even cash collection.

4. **Build for mobile first.** Most Algerian business owners manage operations from their phone, not a laptop.

## When This Doesn't Work

If you're targeting the global market from Algeria, price at global rates (your cost of living is irrelevant to a US customer's willingness to pay).

If you're building for large Algerian enterprises (50+ employees), they pay global SaaS prices because they need enterprise features. The pricing gap only applies to small businesses.

## FAQs

### Q: Can you build a sustainable SaaS at $10/month?
A: If your costs are near zero (free hosting, AI-assisted development, solo founder), yes. You need 100 customers at $10/month for $1,000 MRR, which supports a solo founder in Algeria.

### Q: Should I offer annual discounts?
A: Yes. 2 months free for annual billing. Improves cash flow and reduces churn.

### Q: How do I collect payments from Algerian customers?
A: Baridimob for consumers, CCP/transfer for businesses. For SaaS, you need an automated solution -- Satim or a payment gateway that supports Algerian cards.

### Q: What's the best way to market to Algerian businesses?
A: Facebook groups and WhatsApp. Not ads. Join the groups where your customers are, answer questions, and mention your tool when relevant.
