I have 23 domains in my portfolio. When I started doing cold outreach, I spread my emails across all of them -- 10 emails for FedZip, 10 for ChipWe, 10 for OilHeal, 10 for FinerPay. The result: nothing. Zero replies across the board.

It took me 3,000 emails and 12 months to realize the mistake. I wasn't giving any domain enough attention to actually sell. I was spreading 5% effort across 20 domains when I should have been putting 100% effort into 3.

## The Problem: Dilution Kills Sales

Every domain you own competes for your limited time. Here's the math:

- You have 2 hours per week for outreach
- You own 20 domains
- Each domain gets 6 minutes of attention per week
- At 6 minutes per week, you can't research buyers, personalize emails, or follow up effectively
- Nothing sells

Now here's the alternative:

- You have 2 hours per week for outreach
- You focus on 3 domains
- Each domain gets 40 minutes of attention per week
- You research 5 buyers per domain, write personalized emails, track replies
- Conversation rate goes from zero to something > zero

The difference isn't better domains. It's focus.

## Why Big Portfolios Fail

Domain investors are collectors by nature. We find a good name, register it, feel the dopamine hit, and move to the next one. The portfolio grows. The sales don't.

Three specific traps:

**The "breakthrough" fantasy.** You registered 25 domains hoping one would hit. But a hit requires effort -- no one knocks on your door asking to buy your domain. The domain that sells is the one you actively sell.

**The sunk cost trap.** You paid for 2 years of renewal. Now you feel like you have to hold it until it sells. But the renewal cost is a sunk cost. Holding a domain that isn't selling costs you twice: the renewal fee and the opportunity cost of not focusing on a salable one.

**The spreadsheet illusion.** Organizing your portfolio in a spreadsheet feels productive. It's not. Selling domains requires contacting humans, not organizing data.

## How I Fixed It: The Priority Top 3

I picked my 3 strongest domains using three criteria:

**1. Largest buyer pool.** Which domain has the most companies that could realistically use it?

**2. Highest niche urgency.** Which industry is actively spending money on branding right now?

**3. Best findability of decision-makers.** Which domain lets me find named founders and executives at target companies?

My top 3 came out as:

1. **ScribeClinic.com** -- healthcare is booming, clinic software companies have budgets, every single one has a founder with a name I can find
2. **HomeEligible.com** -- real estate tech is active, affordability tools are trending, named decision-makers everywhere
3. **SlickAgents.com** -- insurance/real estate agent platforms, competitive space, easy to find the people in charge

These three get 100% of my outreach effort until one sells. Then I replace it with #4 from the list.

## How You Can Do It Too

**Step 1:** Rank your portfolio by buyer pool size. Not by how much you like the name. Use the 50-buyer test from [this article](/articles/domains-that-sit-forever/).

**Step 2:** Pick your top 3. Ignore the rest for now. Don't drop them -- just don't spend time on them.

**Step 3:** For each top 3 domain, research 50 named decision-makers at companies that would use it. Founders, CEOs, heads of product. Not "marketing@."

**Step 4:** Send 25 targeted emails per week per domain. Rotate between email providers to maintain deliverability (see [this guide](/articles/domain-cold-emails/) for the infrastructure).

**Step 5:** After 30 days with zero human replies, move that domain to auction and replace it in your top 3.

**Step 6:** Repeat until the portfolio is sold down to a focused 5-10 names.

## When This Doesn't Work

If you own 100+ domains and none of them have a clear buyer pool, focus won't help. You need better acquisition criteria first.

If your domains are all brandables (made-up words), the buyer pool test fails because there's no natural market. Consider dropping these and starting fresh with commercial-intent names.

## FAQs

### Q: How long should I focus on one domain before giving up?
A: 30 days of targeted outbound. If zero human replies after 30 days, the domain is either overpriced, wrong audience, or not compelling enough. Route to auction and move on.

### Q: What if my top 3 domains are weak?
A: That's a signal to improve your acquisition filter. In the meantime, sell the best of what you have, then buy better.

### Q: Should I drop my non-top-3 domains?
A: No. Keep them listed passively (GoDaddy, Afternic, your site). Just don't spend active effort on them. Let them sit until someone finds them or renewal time comes.

### Q: How many domains should I own total?
A: Fewer than you think. A focused portfolio of 10 high-quality domains sells more than a scattered portfolio of 50 average ones.
