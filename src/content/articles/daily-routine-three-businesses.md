I run three income streams: domain investing, freelance web development, and an e-commerce business. All from one laptop, from a town in Algeria where the internet sometimes goes out.

The common question is "how do you manage all three?" The answer is: I don't manage them daily. I manage them on a schedule, with automation handling the rest.

## The Schedule

**Mornings (8-10 AM) -- Domain investing.**

8:00: Check the daily pipeline report (automated script, arrives by email). Review top 5 domains. Register any that pass the 3-criteria test.

8:15: Check domain sales database for comps on my active listings. Update pricing if market data changed.

8:30: Check outreach tracker. See if any replies came in overnight. Classify as auto-reply or human. If human, draft a response.

9:00: Research 10 new contacts for my Top 3 domains. Named decision-makers at relevant companies. Add to outreach list.

9:45: Review portfolio. Any domains hitting their 30-day outbound limit? Route to auction.

Total: 2 hours.

**Mid-morning (10 AM-12 PM) -- Freelance development.**

Client work. Each project has a weekly milestone. I know what I'm building before I sit down because I planned it the day before.

For active projects: write code, review agent output, deploy, update client.

For outreach: respond to inquiries, send proposals, update portfolio with completed projects.

Total: 2 hours.

**Afternoon (12-2 PM) -- E-commerce operations.**

Check the order log. Any new orders? Assign to the team. Confirm addresses. Dispatch pending orders.

Check inventory. Is anything running low? Place restock orders.

Review cash reconciliation from yesterday. Matches the order log? Flag discrepancies.

Brief team meeting (WhatsApp voice, 10 minutes).

Total: 2 hours.

**Evening (2-4 PM) -- Content, planning, learning.**

Write or edit blog posts. Review and approve OmniPost content queue. Catch up on industry reading.

Plan next day's priorities. Update task list.

Total: 2 hours.

## How It Actually Works (vs. The Ideal)

In reality, the schedule looks more like:

- **Good day:** All 4 blocks executed, everything flows
- **Average day:** 3 of 4 blocks, one slips due to client urgency
- **Bad day:** The e-commerce business has a fire (customer complaint, delivery failure, inventory mistake) and everything else waits

The key isn't perfect adherence. It's that the schedule exists as a default path. When nothing is on fire, I follow the schedule. When something needs attention, I deal with it and come back.

## Why Automation Makes This Possible

Without automation, this schedule isn't possible. The domain pipeline alone would take 2 hours of manual checking. The email outreach would consume another 2 hours. Content posting would be another hour.

I wrote about the automation stack in [this article](/articles/automate-everything/), but here's the specific time savings per block:

- Domain block: automation saves 2 hours (pipeline + comps search + dedup)
- Freelance block: AI agents save 2 hours (code generation, testing, deployment)
- E-commerce block: systems save 1 hour (price auto-calculation, order log, reconciliation)
- Content block: OmniPost saves 30 min (auto-posting)

Total automation savings: ~5 hours/day. That's the difference between running 3 businesses and running 1.

## How You Can Do It Too

**Step 1:** Identify your 2-3 income streams. If you have one, that's fine. The framework works for one.

**Step 2:** Define the "default week" for each stream. What needs to happen every day/week/month?

**Step 3:** Automate the 80% that's repetitive. Keep the 20% that needs judgment (see [this framework](/articles/automate-everything/)).

**Step 4:** Schedule your blocks. Morning = deep work. Afternoon = operations and communication.

**Step 5:** Audit weekly. What slipped? Why? Fix the system, not your willpower.

## FAQs

### Q: Isn't 3 businesses too many?
A: Yes, if you're running them all manually. No, if the automation handles execution and you handle decisions. The automation is the multiplier.

### Q: How do I switch context between businesses?
A: The schedule handles this. Each block has a defined focus. I don't check domains during freelance hours.

### Q: What if I don't have 3 businesses yet?
A: Start with one. Build the system. Add more when the first one runs on schedule.

### Q: What's the most important skill for running multiple streams?
A: Saying no. Not to opportunities -- to things that don't fit the schedule. If it doesn't have a block, it doesn't get done.
