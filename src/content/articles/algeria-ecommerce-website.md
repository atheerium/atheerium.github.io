If you run a small business in Algeria and someone told you "you need an e-commerce website," they might be wrong.

A full e-commerce site with product pages, a shopping cart, payment gateway, and order management costs $5,000-15,000 from an agency, or $1,500-3,000 from a freelancer. For most small businesses in Algeria, that investment never pays back.

Meanwhile, WhatsApp + a simple product catalog costs $0 and works immediately.

## The Problem: E-commerce Websites Are Built for a Different Reality

E-commerce sites make sense when:
- Customers browse and buy independently (self-service)
- Payment happens online (credit cards, PayPal)
- Delivery is integrated (automatic label generation, tracking)
- You have inventory in a warehouse with SKUs and barcodes

Most Algerian small businesses don't have these conditions. Their customers:
- Want to talk to someone before buying (trust)
- Pay on delivery (COD, not online)
- Order via WhatsApp because the app is already open
- Ask "do you have this in stock?" before deciding

An e-commerce site in this context becomes a digital brochure, not a sales channel. Customers see the product, then WhatsApp the business to ask about it and order. The website is a middleman, not a transaction point.

## When WhatsApp + Catalog Is Better

If your business looks like this, WhatsApp is all you need:

- **Under 50 orders/day.** A WhatsApp message thread is manageable at this volume.
- **COD only.** Customers pay the delivery person, not online. The website can't process the payment anyway.
- **Personal relationships matter.** Customers buy from people, not from stores. WhatsApp preserves that relationship.
- **You have 1-3 people on the team.** A full e-commerce system adds complexity you don't have the staff for.

**How to do it with zero budget:**
1. Create a WhatsApp Business account (free, adds catalog feature)
2. Add your products to the WhatsApp catalog (photos, prices, descriptions)
3. Post on Facebook/Instagram linking to your WhatsApp
4. When customers message, send the catalog and discuss
5. Track orders in a Google Sheet

**When the volume exceeds this (50+ orders/day):** Add lightweight tools. A shared order management sheet, a delivery price calculator, automated order confirmations. Still no website.

## When You Actually Need a Website

You need a website when:

- Customers find you through Google, not social media (search intent)
- You need to establish credibility (a professional site builds trust)
- You offer services, not products (portfolios, case studies, booking)
- Cash on delivery isn't your primary payment method
- You want to be found in other countries or cities you don't have a physical presence in

A $600-1,500 custom site from a freelancer covers these cases without the $5,000 agency price tag.

## The Middle Ground: Catalog + Simple Site

The best approach for most Algerian small businesses:

- **A one-page site** with: what you sell, pricing, WhatsApp link, delivery information. $300-600 from a freelancer. Gives you credibility without the complexity.
- **Full product operations on WhatsApp.** Orders, questions, support all stay in the channel where customers already are.
- **Upgrade when** customers ask for online ordering or your WhatsApp volume exceeds 50 orders/day.

## How to Decide

**The 3-question test:**
1. Do your customers find you through search or social/suggestions? Search → website. Social → WhatsApp first.
2. Do customers pay online or on delivery? Online → website. COD → WhatsApp.
3. Do you have products that need comparison (specs, variants, reviews)? Yes → website. No → WhatsApp catalog.

If all 3 point to WhatsApp, save your money. If any point to website, invest accordingly.

## FAQs

### Q: Won't I look unprofessional without a website?
A: A one-page site costs $300-600 and fixes this. You don't need a full e-commerce platform. A simple page with your products, WhatsApp link, and delivery info is enough.

### Q: How do I manage high order volume without a website?
A: Google Sheets + a shared WhatsApp group for your team. Add a simple order management tool ($10-15/month) when you exceed 50 orders/day.

### Q: What about payments?
A: COD is standard. For customers who want to pay online, offer Baridimob or CCP transfer. A website doesn't solve the payment problem in Algeria anyway.

### Q: When should I upgrade to a real e-commerce site?
A: When customers consistently ask "can I just order through your website?" or when your order volume exceeds what your team can handle manually, upgrade.
