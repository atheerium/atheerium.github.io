Every Algerian small business asks the same question: should I post in Facebook groups for free, or pay for Meta ads?

I've done both extensively. The answer depends on what you're selling and who you're selling to.

## Facebook Groups: Free but Limited

Facebook groups are the default marketing channel in Algeria. Every city has buy/sell groups with 50,000-500,000 members. Post your product, get inquiries, close the sale on WhatsApp.

**When groups work well:**
- **New business with zero budget.** You can start selling today with just a phone and product photos.
- **Physical products with visual appeal.** Clothing, home decor, cosmetics. Things people want to see and share.
- **Local audience.** Groups are organized by city/wilaya. Perfect for businesses that deliver locally.
- **Test a product idea.** Post in a group, see how many people express interest. If engagement is high, invest in ads.

**When groups don't work well:**
- **Service businesses.** "I build websites" posts get 2 likes. People don't buy services from group posts.
- **High-ticket items.** A 50,000 DZD product in a group post looks suspicious. Groups are for small purchases.
- **Scalability.** You can only post 1-2 times per day before you get flagged as spam. Limited reach.

**My experience:** Groups got me my first 50 orders. They're the best way to start. But I hit a ceiling at about 10 orders/day. Beyond that, I needed ads.

## Meta Ads: Paid but Scalable

Meta ads (Facebook and Instagram) cost money but let you target specific audiences, scale your reach, and track results.

**When ads work well:**
- **Proven products.** You already know the product sells (validated in groups). Now you want volume.
- **Specific targeting.** Age, location, interests, behaviors. You can reach exactly the right customer.
- **Retargeting.** Someone visited your catalog but didn't order? Show them an ad. This converts at 3-5x cold traffic.
- **Brand building.** Even if people don't buy immediately, they see your name. Next time they need what you sell, they remember you.

**When ads don't work well:**
- **Untested products.** You don't know if people want it. Ads amplify the problem faster.
- **Low margin products.** If your profit is 200 DZD per order, one bad ad campaign wipes out a week of profits.
- **Small budget.** Less than 500 DZD/day won't generate enough data to optimize. Groups are better at this budget.

**My experience:** At 500 DZD/day, I get about 5-10 orders directly from ads. The breakeven point depends on margin. For a product with 500 DZD profit, I need a 10% conversion rate to break even.

## The Strategy That Works

**Phase 1: Validate in groups (0-50 orders).**
- Post in 3-5 relevant groups daily
- Track which products get the most engagement
- Build a customer base and get reviews
- Don't spend on ads yet

**Phase 2: Test with small ad budget (50-200 orders).**
- Spend 300-500 DZD/day on the best-selling product from Phase 1
- Target the same audience that engaged with group posts
- Track cost per order
- If cost per order is below your profit per order, scale up

**Phase 3: Scale with ads (200+ orders).**
- Increase ad budget to 1,000-2,000 DZD/day
- Expand to new products that tested well
- Use retargeting for people who engaged but didn't buy
- Reduce group posting (maintain presence but don't prioritize)

## How You Can Do It Too

1. Join 5 active buy/sell groups in your wilaya
2. Post your best product with clear photos and price
3. Move serious inquiries to WhatsApp
4. Track which products get the most interest
5. Invest 50% of first month's profit into Meta ads for the top product
6. Scale what works, cut what doesn't

## FAQs

### Q: How much should I spend on Meta ads in Algeria?
A: Start at 300 DZD/day ($2). If it works, scale to 1,000 DZD/day. Most Algerian businesses overspend. Start small and increase.

### Q: Which Facebook groups should I join?
A: "بيع و شراء الجزائر" type groups for your city. Also niche groups related to your product category.

### Q: Can I run ads without a Facebook page?
A: No. You need a Facebook Page to run Meta ads. Set it up before you start.

### Q: How do I track if my ads are working?
A: Use Meta Pixel on your website, or track manually: "customer said they saw my ad" → check your ad dashboard for that period.
