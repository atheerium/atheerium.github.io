Two strategies for selling domains: list them on Afternic/GoDaddy and wait for offers, or proactively email potential buyers.

I've tried both for 12 months. Here's the honest comparison:

- **Afternic listing:** 2 lowball offers in 12 months ($50 and $100). Zero sales.
- **Cold outreach:** 3,000 emails sent. Zero sales. But ~5% human reply rate after improving targeting.

Neither converted to a sale. But one approach has a path to revenue and the other is waiting.

## Afternic Listing: Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- One-time setup (list once, forget it)
- Passive (the marketplace does the work)
- Exposure to buyers you'd never find through outbound

**Cons:**
- Most domains get zero views
- Lowball offers ($50-100 on domains priced $299+)
- Afternic takes 15-20% commission
- No control over timing

**Verdict:** Worth doing because it costs nothing except setup time. But don't expect sales. Take the passive exposure, invest your active effort elsewhere.

## Cold Outreach: Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- You control the timing
- You choose who sees the domain
- Targeted messaging (you can explain the value)
- Higher potential price (buyer sees the value, not just a listing)

**Cons:**
- High effort per domain (research + writing + sending)
- Requires email infrastructure to avoid spam (see [this guide](/articles/domain-cold-emails/))
- 95%+ of emails get no human reply
- Requires organized tracking (who you emailed, when, what they said)

**Verdict:** Higher effort, higher ceiling. The 5% who reply are worth more than any Afternic inquiry I've received.

## The Real Strategy: Both, With the Right Priority

Here's the framework I use now:

**1. Always list on Afternic/GoDaddy.** It takes 10 minutes per domain. The exposure is free. Lowball offers tell you something about market perception. Accept or counter -- either way, you learn.

**2. Invest 80% of effort in cold outreach.** The replies, even the rejections, teach you something about pricing, messaging, and buyer psychology. The one sale will justify the effort.

**3. Monitor both channels weekly.** Did anyone view the Afternic listing? Did anyone reply to an email? Data tells you what to do next.

**4. Route to auction** if both channels produce nothing after 30-90 days.

## Data and Awareness

**Domains that attract unsolicited offers on Afternic:**
- Dictionary word .coms (common English words)
- Geo domains (city + keyword)
- Short (4-5L) domains
These are the exception, not the rule.

**Domains that benefit from cold outreach:**
- Industry-specific brandables (FedZip for logistics)
- Problem-solution names (HomeEligible for real estate tech)
- Any domain where you can find 50+ companies that would logically want it

Match the channel to the domain type.

## How You Can Do It Too

1. **List every domain** on Afternic with a BIN price from your pricing tier.
2. **For domains with a clear buyer pool,** invest 30 days of outbound.
3. **For domains without clear buyers** (brandables, short names), rely on Afternic and accept lower offers.
4. **Track both channels.** Which domains get views? Which get replies? The data improves your acquisition criteria.

## FAQs

### Q: Should I use Sedo or Afternic?
A: Afternic/GoDaddy has the largest buyer pool. Sedo for international buyers. Use both.

### Q: What's a reasonable Afternic price for a $299 domain?
A: $299 BIN is fine. Afternic takes 15-20%, so you net ~$250.

### Q: How do I handle lowball Afternic offers?
A: Counter at your BIN price. If they decline, you learned the market price. Accept it and move on.

### Q: Can I do both at the same time?
A: Yes. List on Afternic while doing outreach. The Afternic listing is passive. The outreach is active.
