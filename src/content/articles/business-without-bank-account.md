Every online business person in Algeria hits the same wall: you can't receive international payments through normal banking.

PayPal lets you send but not receive. Bank cards get blocked for international transactions. Wire transfers take 2 weeks and cost 5,000 DZD in fees. The global payment infrastructure wasn't built for us.

But there's a workaround that works. I've been using it for a year. It's not perfect, but it's reliable.

## The Problem: Algeria's Banking System Is a One-Way Valve

Here's what doesn't work for receiving international payments as an Algerian freelancer:

- **PayPal:** You can receive, but can't withdraw to an Algerian bank account. The money sits in PayPal forever.
- **Bank wire (SWIFT):** Works but takes 7-14 days, costs 5,000-10,000 DZD in intermediary fees, and requires justification documents.
- **Western Union:** You can receive, but the exchange rate is terrible and they limit amounts.
- **Credit cards:** Most Algerian cards (CIB, EDBBiya, CCP) don't work for international online payments. They're blocked by default.

The result: you can earn money online, but getting it into your hands in DZD is a puzzle.

## The Solution: USDT via Binance P2P

The most reliable method I've found is a three-step process:

**Step 1: Receive USDT.** The client sends payment in USDT (stablecoin pegged to USD) to my Binance wallet. Most international freelancers and domain buyers can do this. It takes 2 minutes and costs $0-2 in network fees.

**Step 2: Convert to DZD via P2P.** On Binance P2P, I list an offer to sell USDT. An Algerian buyer transfers DZD to my bank account (or CCP/Baridimob). I release the USDT. The trade completes in 10-30 minutes.

**Step 3: Withdraw to my bank.** The DZD is now in my Algerian bank account, available to spend.

**Fees:**
- USDT transfer: $1-2 (TRC-20 network, cheapest)
- Binance P2P spread: 1-2% (you sell slightly below market rate)
- Total cost: ~2-3% vs 5-10% for bank wire + exchange rate losses

**Speed:** Client sends Monday morning. Money hits my Algerian account Monday afternoon.

## What About Local Payments?

For Algerian clients paying in DZD, it's simpler:

- **CCP (postal transfer):** Free, reliable, takes 1-3 days. Best for larger amounts.
- **Baridimob (EDDBiya):** Instant transfers under 200,000 DZD. Works like a debit card.
- **Cash:** For local clients, cash is still king.

For recurring payments (SaaS subscriptions), none of these automate well. I'm still working on a solution for this.

## What About Wise, Payoneer, or Skrill?

- **Wise:** Doesn't support Algeria as a receiving country. You can hold multiple currencies but can't withdraw to a DZD account.
- **Payoneer:** Supports Algeria but requires a physical card and has high conversion fees.
- **Skrill:** Works for receiving but withdrawal to Algerian banks is unreliable.

Binance P2P has been more reliable than any of these for me.

## How You Can Do It Too

1. Create a Binance account (you can verify with an Algerian passport)
2. Buy a small amount of USDT to test the process
3. Find a buyer on P2P with good ratings (99%+ completion rate)
4. Send a small amount first to verify the process works
5. Once confirmed, use this as your primary receiving method

**Security warning:** Only trade with verified buyers who have 99%+ completion and 500+ trades. Don't accept P2P offers that seem too good on the rate. Scammers target new users.

## When This Doesn't Work

If your clients can't use cryptocurrency (some corporate policies prohibit it), you need a different approach. Options: Wise (if they send to your Wise account, you can hold USD and spend via Wise card), or a US-based friend who converts for you.

If you need to receive small amounts (under $50), the network fees for USDT make it not worth it. Use Baridimob transfers from local clients instead.

## FAQs

### Q: Is Binance legal in Algeria?
A: Cryptocurrency trading is in a legal grey area. There's no law against it, but there's also no regulatory framework. I know dozens of Algerians using it without issues.

### Q: What about taxes?
A: I report my income as freelance earnings through the normal tax system. The crypto conversion is just a currency exchange, not a taxable event.

### Q: What's the best network for USDT transfers?
A: TRC-20 (Tron network). Fees are $1-2 and confirmation takes 2 minutes. Don't use Ethereum (ERC-20) which costs $10-50 per transfer.

### Q: Will this work for large amounts ($5,000+)?
A: Yes, but split into smaller trades on P2P. One $5,000 trade is harder to fill than five $1,000 trades.
